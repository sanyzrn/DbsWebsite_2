Building an AI feature is easy to romanticize and hard to ship. The useful question is rarely
"can we call a model?" — it's "what decision or workflow becomes meaningfully better when a
model is in the loop?"

## Start from the workflow

Most early AI demos look impressive in isolation and collapse when they meet auth, billing,
edge cases, and the boring parts of a real product. Anchor the work in a concrete user path
first; then decide whether a model is the right lever.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Rule of thumb">
  If you cannot describe the before/after of the workflow in one sentence, you are not ready
  to choose a model — you are still clarifying the product.
</Callout>

## Where integration earns its keep

Useful AI layers tend to sit next to:

- repetitive judgment calls with clear inputs/outputs
- retrieval over *your* documents, not the open web
- drafts that a human still reviews before anything ships
