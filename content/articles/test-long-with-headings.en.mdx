This draft is a layout stress fixture for Field Notes. It is intentionally longer than a
typical short note so reading-time calculation, heading rhythm, and mid-article spacing can
be reviewed without inventing a real essay. Treat every sentence as placeholder texture for
design QA — not as advice you would publish.

## Why long fixtures still matter

Short samples hide problems that only show up after a few scrolls. A long piece forces the
prose container to reveal max-width comfort, paragraph spacing, and whether H2s create a
clear outline when someone skims. If the page feels monotonous here, it will feel worse with
real content that actually asks for attention.

When evaluating, open this URL on mobile and desktop, then jump between the headings below.
Confirm the sticky header clearance still leaves the first heading readable, and that the
breadcrumb / meta row do not collide with the fixed nav on small screens.

## Structure the reader can skim

A useful long article usually exposes its shape early. For this fixture, the shape is fake
but still hierarchical: an opening, a few section titles, a list of concrete checks, and a
blockquote that should visually interrupt the column without looking like an error banner.

Use this section to judge whether H2 size, weight, and top margin feel intentional next to
body copy. If headings look timid, bump them in the prose stylesheet — do not pad the MDX
with extra blank lines as a workaround.

## Checklist for layout review

When you open this fixture, walk through:

- reading-time label matches roughly 800–1000 English words
- each H2 is easy to spot while scrolling quickly
- the bullet list below keeps consistent indent and line-height
- the blockquote has enough contrast without stealing the page

Concrete checks:

- Title and description still feel secondary to the brand, not louder than the site chrome
- Body measure stays near 70ch so lines do not stretch on wide monitors
- Related-projects / related-articles asides (when present) sit below a clear rule
- Draft status keeps this out of `/articles` listings and sitemap generation

## A pause in the middle

> Layout fixtures should look obviously temporary. If a visitor could mistake this for a
> published Field Note, the draft pipeline or visual treatment is too quiet about its role.

After the quote, keep reading for filler paragraphs that exist only to lengthen the scroll.
They deliberately repeat ideas about testing article chrome: spacing, hierarchy, and the
difference between a card on the index and a full reading column on the detail route.

Imagine you are reviewing a pull request that only changes typography. You need enough text
to see widows, cramped lists, and heading collisions. You also need enough text to notice
when the page transition or decorative grid distracts from reading. That is the only job of
the remaining paragraphs.

Another pass: scan for uneven gaps between sections. If one H2 feels glued to the previous
paragraph while another floats, the prose CSS is inconsistent. Fix the stylesheet, then
re-check this fixture before touching real articles.

Keep going a little further so the reading-time estimate climbs. Placeholder sentences about
layout QA are fine here because status stays draft. Production content elsewhere on the site
must remain specific and honest; these three test articles are the explicit exception called
out for design evaluation.

Finally, end with a calm closing paragraph. A long fixture should not climax — it should
simply stop once it has exercised headings, a list, a quote, and enough words for timing.
When you are done reviewing, leave the files as draft and move on to the next fixture that
covers code and callouts.
